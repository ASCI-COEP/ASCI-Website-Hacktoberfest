Interview Diaries

Sanket Patil

Computer Engineering, Batch of '21
Veritas Technologies LLC.
  1. How do you structure your Resume?
    • Most common structure of resume is single page having two columns.
    • Resume should be occupied with technical sections as much as possible like Experience, Project, Educational background, Technical Skills, Achievements, Profiles etc.
    • With key points like name, contact details, experience(if any) should be easily noticeable.
  2. What are some good resources to prepare for Coding Sections of Aptitude Tests?
    • I prefer practice on leetcode when there is less time to prepare ( less than couple of months).Focus on easy medium level of difficulty on LC.
    • Having strong competitive coding profiles is big plus when it comes to coding sections of apti.
    • Also I think be familiar to HackerRank interface because most of companies(specially on-campus) prefer taking test on it.
  3. What projects should you have on your CV?
    • There is nothing like everyone should have projects from specific domains or all domains.
    • But whatever project you put on resume, you should be comfortable to explain that in details.
  4. In Interviews, what questions are asked based on your Projects?
    • Questions related to project depends on interviewer.
    • Sometimes just explanation of project is sufficient but many times interviewer goes in details about what exactly you did in project, what are your technical learnings, basic design of project, problems faced during implementation etc.
    • Also interviewer can ask you questions about technology you worked on project assuming you have experience working on it.
  5. Some tips for interviews?
    • You must be confident on your learnings and whatever you have on resume.
    • Do some analysis whenever you face a rejection and try to improve on areas where you went wrong.
    • In case of on-campus process, try to recover from rejection as quickly as possible.
    • Be prepared before placement season starts( on-campus) because you hardly get little time to prepare when companies are lined up each day.
  6. Do Fest/ Club Participation and Roles really matter?
    • Depends :)
  7. How important are letter of recommendations? Who should you get them from and how?
    • For off-campus placement drives referrals are much important to get you test link/interview call.
    • Best way to get referrals is grow you community on platforms like LinkedIn.
  8. What are the prime points to prepare for before going to an interview?
    • Prepare your resume well and know each and every point you mentioned on your resume in detail.
    • Study well about work areas and technologies used in that specific company (which you are targeting).
    • Always try to end interview on good note and I think chance to do that is when interviewer asks you about your question for them at the end.
  9. What is the flow of the interview?
    • Again flow of interview depends on interviewer.
    • In most of the cases interviews starts with your introduction and questions on resumes.
    • Also in most interviews algo/coding questions are kept at the last. But I think there is no any fixed flow for interview.
