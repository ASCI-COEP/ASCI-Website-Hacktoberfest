Interview Diaries

Yash Jakhotiya

Computer Engineering, Batch of '20
DE Shaw & Co.
  1. How do you structure your Resume?
    • Experience first, followed by project work, then education, and finally technical skills.
  2. What are some good resources to prepare for Coding Sections of Aptitude Tests?
    • From Codeforces to Leetcode to Geeksforgeeks, anything is fine as long as you actually DO!
    • More helpful if it is with a schedule.
  3. What projects should you have on your CV?
    • Anything you can talk about thoroughly
    • It is preferable to have projects relevant to the job profile you are applying for, but honestly, get started with anything you are comfortable with, and build things.
  4. In Interviews, what questions are asked based on your Projects?
    • A good pre-interview exercise would be to think how you can improve an existing project and make it more efficient or more scalable.
    • Can it be deployed on a cloud infrastructure where it is easier to scale it? Can you change your architecture to reduce response times?
  5. Some tips for interviews?
    • Be calm. Understand and imbibe the incoming question first, before you start outputting an answer.
    • Don't just sit while you develop a solution, think out loud. It helps when they can understand where you are going with an approach.
    • Tailor your resume to the job you are applying.
  6. Do Fest/ Club Participation and Roles really matter?
    • For fests, as far as I can tell, the simple answer is NO in terms of job placements.However, it is tough to quantify the soft skills you develop there.
    • Nevertheless, having a technical project to talk about on your resume could be better than having a fest participation.
    • Clubs matter only when they are technical and you can show the impact and the output of your own work.
  7. How important are letter of recommendations? Who should you get them from and how?
    • For job placements, I haven't come across a requirement for LORs. However, for higher studies you do require them.
    • As a general rule, try to get them from someone who can say that they worked with on you on some XX project or work, and that you showed YY qualities which they believe will help you succeed in the place you are applying to.
  8. What are the prime points to prepare for before going to an interview?
    • Expect questions on your resume. Prepare to sell it.
    • Be prepared for the fact that they may skip your resume altogether, so don't fret if they do.
  9. What is the flow of the interview?
    • Really depends on the interviewer.
    • Some like to cover depth when exploring a topic. Some like to cover breadth.
    • Be prepared for both.
