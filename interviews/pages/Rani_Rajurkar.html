Interview Diaries

Rani Rajurkar

Computer Engineering, Batch of '20
VMware
  1. How do you structure your Resume?
    • Modify your resume according to the job description, try not to have generic resume everywhere.
    • Keep a one pager Resume, you can use standard templates.
  2. What are some good resources to prepare for Coding Sections of Aptitude Tests?
    • Leetcode, Cracking the Coding interview and Dynamic Programming series by Aditya Verma are some of the good resources.
  3. What projects should you have on your CV?
    • Include important and relevant projects with crisp explanation, you can use STAR method to describe.
    • Try avoiding long paragraphs.
  4. In Interviews, what questions are asked based on your Projects?
    • Expect questions like what technologies you used and why, your contribution, difficulties faced during the implementation and questions around different use cases.
  5. Some tips for interviews?
    • Think out loud, communicate with the interviewer and make your approach heard confidently
    • Do not bluff, be honest with your answers everywhere, you don't have to know it all.
  6. Do Fest/ Club Participation and Roles really matter?
    • Technical Clubs, yes they matter. You can be asked about your role and experience.
  7. How important are letter of recommendations? Who should you get them from and how?
    • They matter only for Higher studies and not really for Jobs.
    • Try to get them from faculties/mentors you've directly worked under.
  8. What are the prime points to prepare for before going to an interview?
    • Go through all the things you have listed on your resume thoroughly.
    • Brush up core concepts, basic algorithms, puzzles. Also check out past experiences from GFG.
  9. What is the flow of the interview?
    • Aptitude, Technical round 1, Technical round 2, Managerial(Behavioural + Technical) and HR.
